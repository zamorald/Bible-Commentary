Therefore an overseer must be above reproach, the husband of one wife, sober-minded, self-controlled, respectable, hospitable, able to teach,

husband of one wife

The Greek here (μιᾶς γυναικὸς ἄνδρα) emphasizes the number "one" since polygamy was very common in the region.
Resist the urge to interpret this requirement as only married once; Greek has a way of denoting repetitive action, and it isn't used here.
A man remains qualified, for example, though twice married if he survived his first wife and has married again. A divorced but remarried man remains qualified if he divorced his first wife on the grounds of fornication (Matthew 19:9).
Remember the rule: these requirements are simply specifics of faitfhulness. Every married person must be like the elder in this respect. If we have any husband--whether he desires to be an elder or not--who is not the "husband of one wife," we must withdraw from him because he is in sin (1 Corinthians 5:4-5).
