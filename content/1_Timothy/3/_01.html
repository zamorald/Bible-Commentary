If anyone aspires

It is excellent when a person desires to serve the Lord and His people, and especially when a person is willing to work in the capacity of an elder. We must remember the admonition of the Holy Spirit if we wish to encourage men to serve in this office:

Hebrews 13:17

Obey your leaders and submit to them, for they are keeping watch over your souls, as those who will have to give an account. Let them do this with joy and not with groaning, for that would be of no advantage to you.

The (Dis)Qualifications of Elders

The qualities listed here are intended to remind us what is qualified Christian living so that we can recognize those among us who are approved by God.

A word of warning is in order since Satan would love nothing better than to convince the church we cannot and should not have elders. Too often, this list is used to disqualify men who wish to serve rather than as a reminder of the qualities we seek. The qualifications turn into disqualifications when people interpret this list in such unreasonable terms that no one can satisfy them.

These requirements are simply specifics of faitfhulness. They remind us of the qualities that characterize a life of faith. They are not intended to be tricky or impossible to attain--at least, not any more so than it is to remain faithful to God.

On closer inspection, the qualities described here are applicable generally. That is, there isn't any quality in the list that is not required of every Christian.

Obviously, we do not mean that every Christian is required to be a man, a husband, a father. Not every Christian must be those things or even can be those things, although an elder certainly must.

But if a Christian is a husband, this is the kind of husband he must be if he is to be right with God. If he is a father, this is the kind of father he must be.

Consider the items in this list. What Christian is allowed not to be "above reproach?" What Christian is allowed to practice polygamy? To be drunk, out of control, shameful, rude, unable to tell someone else how to be saved? To get in fist fights and loud arguments? To love money?

If a Christian is a father, but he is not an elder, is he therefore allowed to let his children go out of control?

What Christian is allowed to stay immature forever? Or, what Christian is allowed to be puffed up with conceit? What Christian can be right with God and be an infamous criminal at the same time?

If we have any among us who walk in any of these sins, we are obligated to discipline them (1 Corinthians 5:4-5). This list does not define an unattainable super-man to be appointed as elder. It requires a faithful husband and father who has demonstrated (by means of his children) that people can obey the gospel under his leadership. That's hard enough.
